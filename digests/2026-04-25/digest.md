**Substack**
Antitrust victories feel satisfying until you realize enforcement is the harder half. The Live Nation ruling broke up a monopoly on paper — but the infrastructure of venue exclusivity, service fees, and market lock-in doesn't dissolve with a verdict. Vox does the work of tempering the celebration: structural remedies take years, new entrants face the same barriers, and the culture of extraction in live music is baked in well below the corporate level. The ruling matters. What happens next matters more.
